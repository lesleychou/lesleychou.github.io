About Me

I am currently a PhD student at UMD College Park, advised by Prof. Alan (Zaoxing) Liu . We are working on system, networking, and security problems with exciting data-driven approaches.

I am also honored to be advised by Prof. Junchen Jiang from University of Chicago and Dr. Francis Yan from Microsoft Research (MSR) prior to the PhD program. We proposed Genet for improving the performance and generalization of RL-based networking systems.

I received my M.S. degree from KAIST, majoring in Electrical Engineering. I received my B.S. degree from Xidian University in China, majoring in Computer Science.

Genet: Automatic Curriculum Generation for Learning Adaptation in Networking
Zhengxu Xia*, Yajie Zhou*, Francis Y. Yan, Junchen Jiang (*co-first author)
ACM SIGCOMM August 2022.
Link to talk: SIGCOMM-Genet

Summary: We proposed Genet, a novel training framework that enhances the performance and generalization of reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms in networking and systems. Genet builds on curriculum learning with judicious use of rule-based baselines. It substantially improves the performance and generalization of simulation-trained RL algorithms under unseen workloads and in real environments.
