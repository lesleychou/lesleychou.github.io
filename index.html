About:

I am currently a PhD student at Boston University, advised by Prof. Alan Liu . We are working on an exciting Network-telemetry data privacy project with Redhat research .

I am also honored to be advised by Prof. Junchen Jiang from University of Chicago and Dr. Francis Yan from Microsoft Research (MSR). We focus on improving the performance and generalization of RL-based networking systems.

Before joining Boston University, I received my M.S. degree from KAIST, majoring in Electrical Engineering. I received my B.S. degree from Xidian University majoring in Computer Science.


Publication:

Automatic Curriculum Generation for Learning Adaptation in Networking
Zhengxu Xia*, Yajie Zhou*, Francis Y. Yan, Junchen Jiang (*equal contribution)
To appear at ACM SIGCOMM, August 2022.

Summary: We proposed Genet, a novel training framework that enhances the performance and generalization of reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms in networking and systems. Genet builds on curriculum learning with judicious use of rule-based baselines. It substantially improves the performance and generalization of simulation-trained RL algorithms under unseen workloads and in real environments.
